Placement Fundae

October 31, 2019

It will come and go as a storm. Just be prepared and don't let it take away your calmness.

Hey. I am Akash Jain and somehow completed my bachelors from Department of Aerospace Engineering, IIT Kanpur. If you have not planned yet and waiting for Day 1 to knock, do give this a read. I have listed a few ways to approach the season smartly and bring out the best from it.

Hello guys!! Hope you are doing well. I am Akash Jain, a 2019 pass out from the department of aerospace engineering. Currently, I am working as a software engineer in Societe Generale.

As the placement season is approaching, I would like to share my experience and some points to help you through the placements. Firstly I would say you have to play smart and strategize well. To start with, it is the resume. You should get your resume reviewed by people from different domains, having different interests and expertise. Once your resume is done then comes shortlisting companies. Choose companies you would like to apply to but be careful not to apply to companies way out of your league or on the bottom side. Keep in mind the selection criterion of the company and whether you meet the requirements like CPI cutoff and other such necessary stuff. While selecting keep in mind the profile you are applying for and consider if you have some background or expertise in that field. Consult your seniors while making the list as they are the best ones to guide you. I would suggest you make 3 lists, the best-case scenario, the average case, and the worst-case, but make sure these are the companies you would be happy to go if you are selected. Above the best criteria, you may apply or you may leave them. In case you are certain you won’t get shortlisted for the big firms, just leave them. Again guys, don’t get stressed unnecessarily seeing people around you getting selected in those companies. Peer pressure can lower your self-confidence. Be patient:)

It’s not like your best case scenario companies will come first, then the average case followed by your worst case. Your worst-case scenario company might come on Day 1 slot and your best scenario company might come on Day 3.You have to decide based on your chances and your preparation level. Get a rough idea of the Days and slots of the companies. In case you decide you don’t want to go to a company, don’t apply for it. It might also happen that you did not apply for your worst-case scenario on Day 1 and you get rejected by your best case scenario on Day 2. So always keep a backup for yourself. Optimize companies according to your preferences.

Coming to the companies in your best-case scenario, give the highest priority to these companies. While shortlisting also categorize based on the type of the company, like core or non-core, software development profile or business analyst profile, as each profile will require a different kind of preparation. Prepare hard for best-case scenarios such that you also cover your average and worst-case scenarios as your best companies would require you to prepare hardest. Get to know the tests, requirements, the company’s selection process, interview rounds. You should go through the SPO preparation portal and try contacting seniors who have gone through the company’s selection process. Once you get an idea of what the company’s demands are, prepare accordingly.

Now comes the question from where to prepare for the interview. In almost all of my interviews, the resume was thoroughly verified. Be careful about what you write. Whenever you go for an interview, you have to prepare an introduction. You can give an overview of the things already mentioned in your resume or mention your other achievements, maybe in extracurriculars. The interviewer can select something from your resume he finds interesting. They are usually catchy about the numbers and figures in your resume. Puzzles are also asked in interviews. There are some popular sites for puzzle-solving and they can ask questions directly from these websites. Here’s a trick for you guys!! Even if you have solved the question before, take some time to think about it and most importantly talk to the interviewer about your approach. Talking to the interviewer shows that you have good communication skills and you are a team person. For the HR round, I would suggest you document something so that you have a list of the HR questions and the answers to the questions. You won’t be able to concentrate well just before the interview as there will be a lot of things going on in your mind at that time and you won’t be able to say all that you want to in that stressful situation. Another thing usually asked in business analyst and consulting profiles are guesstimates which can be practiced from various online platforms. For GDs as well, go through online resources, see few GDs and the tricks involved, they do work actually. In one of the GDs, the interviewers were noting down the names of the candidates who spoke first. So one of the tips for GDs is to speak first. For technical rounds, talk to your seniors and go through the job description. Some companies may ask the questions asked by bigger firms in their own rounds. Except the Day 1 and Day 2 companies, most of the companies ask questions asked by the bigger firms in their previous interviews. Don’t leave those companies thinking that the questions will be very complex. You stand a good chance!!

The part people bother about the most is undoubtedly CPI and more so in case it’s not up to the mark. Your academic requirements will depend on the profile you are applying for. So I would say don’t get too stressed, the CPI is just a qualifying criterion. If you have CPI above the qualifying criteria you will be treated equally as other candidates having higher CPI than you in the interview. The CPI criterion is different for different companies, some have it explicitly mentioned while others might keep it open to all. Both CPI and extracurriculars are valued by companies. A good CPI with extracurriculars definitely gives you an edge over others. You should have something other than your curriculum but if you have done some good course projects you can also mention that in your resume. Some companies would require you to have some PoRs so that they can ensure you have the team spirit and leadership skills needed for that profile. Questions like ‘Why do you have a low CPI?’ can be asked in case your CPI is low. Think of some way to justify it. You can use your extracurricular achievements or other projects to justify it but make sure you are able to handle the counter questions. It’s all about facing their questions in a smart way such that your low CPI does not give the interviewers a negative impression of you. Again a good CPI just ensures that you will definitely get an interview call but does not guarantee you the offer letter. So whether your CPI is high or low, if you have got the call you can be sure, you have equal chances as anybody else. What solely matters is your preparation and confidence level on the D-day and congrats!! you have that dream job. Don’t get stressed because of your CPI and focus solely on your performance in the interview. Always remember you are not alone with the problems you face during the placements which could be low CPI or anything else. You can google to get answers to most of your problems but remember there are both pros and cons to it. So it’s suggested to select the answers suitable to your profile and get it verified from your seniors.

For students who are not sitting for placements this season, your first task will be to check whether you have sufficient material to fill up one page. In case you don’t have sufficient material try for activities in different domains or if you have some clarity about what field you want to go in try to do some activities in that field. You can also go for some activities that show a different personality trait of yours. Team spirit is what many companies look for, so being in some activity will add value to your resume. But again, I would suggest that don’t do an activity just to fill in your resume. The interviewer can see the duration for which you have been involved in an activity and is likely to judge your consistency in case you have switched roles frequently. This could suggest that you are going to leave the company in the future which no recruiter wants.

I would pen down a few of my interview experiences so that people can have some idea. In one of my interviews, somehow the recruiter got an impression that I would go for an MBA in the future and I was even asked directly. So it’s not only important to answer the questions but also think from the interviewer’s perspective. I did not like the job location and type of work that would be given to me in another interview. So after the 2nd round, I decided to back off. In the 4th round, I told the interviewers that I was not interested so they did not shortlist me. While interacting with the interviewers in one of the rounds I chose a wrong data structure. The interviewer explained to me the correct data structure and asked me to approach the problem using that data structure. Although I knew the data structure from before I asked for some time to think and came up with the solution which impressed the interviewer. In one of the GDs, I knew I had to start the discussion but I somehow missed it. I could see the person managing the GD write down the names of the people who started the talk and I panicked. But I noted down the important points others missed and spoke when I got the chance. The duration one is speaking and the content matter in a GD. I remember a guy who was speaking a lot in the GD didn’t get selected. In another interview the interviewer was shifting between topics very quickly like from hardware to architecture to algorithms. He asked me whether I knew about the topics before asking questions from them. So it’s always recommended that you be honest if you don’t know some topics otherwise it creates a very bad impression if you give a wrong answer. Again when multiple people are good at a thing the interviewer might look for something extra in you.

I would conclude by saying that the placement season is going to be very stressful. Whether you are sitting for placement or not it will be very important for you. After Day 7 or 8, there is a large gap between the number of students sitting for the tests and those who are yet to be placed. Talk to people and don’t get stressed unnecessarily. Try to find out areas where you are lacking. Find some seniors who have the same resume as yours and ask them how they approached the placement season. Get to know everything from them as they can guide you the best. I remember my seniors and wingmates were always there to help. Be there for your friends and try to help them as much as possible.

My views are from my own personal experiences. Other people’s views are also invited in case somebody wants to add any more points other than these. Wishing all of you good luck for your future. Cheers!!

- Akash Jain
